# Cross-AI Plan Review — Phase 4.5

## Gemini Review

### 1. Summary
Phase 4.5 introduces critical conversion mechanisms (14-day Stripe trial, free leads counter, qualification scoring, and paywall analytics). The plans are highly structured, featuring idempotent database migrations and a comprehensive threat model that actively mitigates privilege escalation and client-side data tampering. 

### 2. Strengths
- **Idempotent Migrations:** Excellent use of `IF NOT EXISTS` and `ENABLE ROW LEVEL SECURITY` in 04.5-01 to ensure safe schema upgrades.
- **Data Integrity:** Smart use of Zod in 04.5-04 to strictly filter `checkout_completed` events, ensuring they can only be written by the secure Stripe webhook.
- **Decoupled Architecture:** Trial logic correctly offloads trial-end notifications and double-trial prevention entirely to Stripe.

### 3. Concerns
- **HIGH:** `qualify_score` is computed on the client and accepted as "informational" (T-04.5-04), yet it is used to filter critical business analytics in 04.5-04 (`qualify_score >= 3`). A malicious user could forge this score to skew conversion data.
- **MEDIUM:** The mitigation for DoS on `paywall-events.post.ts` (T-04.5-13) relies on global middleware (INFRA-04) which is marked as pending in `PROJECT.md`. If missing, the database could be spammed.

### 4. Suggestions
- **Recalculate Score Server-Side:** Do not trust the client for `qualify_score`. Calculate it in the backend based on the raw project details (budget provided, phone provided, etc.) before writing to `projects` and `paywall_events`.
- **Local Rate Limiting:** Implement a basic per-IP/per-pro rate limiter directly inside `paywall-events.post.ts` using `lru-cache` or similar until INFRA-04 is verified.

### 5. Risk Assessment
**MEDIUM RISK.** The architectural foundation is solid, but the reliance on potentially forged client data (`qualify_score`) for executive analytics could lead to poor business decisions. Securing the score computation is necessary before launch.
